## PART 1: Facebook Posts Designed to Generate Commenters

The strategy: ask a question that laser operators genuinely want to answer. Every person who comments becomes a warm DM target because you can reference their specific answer.

### Post Option A: "What material humbled you?"

```
Question for the group:

What material gave you the hardest time dialing in? The one where you burned through stock, wasted gas, and still couldn't get a clean edge?

For me it was mirror polished stainless. Thought I had it figured out, changed suppliers, and everything I knew went out the window.

What's yours?
```

**Why this works:**
1. Everyone has a "war story" material. It's emotionally charged so people WANT to share.
2. Comments will include specific materials, thicknesses, and machine details (perfect DM opener).
3. Low barrier: one sentence answer is enough.
4. Not selling anything. Pure commiseration.

**DM hook afterward:** "hey [name], saw your comment about [material]. did you ever figure it out or still fighting it?"

---

### Post Option B: "What's your test cut ritual?"

```
Curious how other people handle this:

When you get a material you've never cut before, what's your first move? Do you have a system or do you just start somewhere and adjust?

I've been doing line tests at 10% speed increments but wondering if there's a faster way to get in the ballpark. What's your process look like?
```

**Why this works:**
1. Positions you as someone learning (non-threatening, invites advice giving).
2. Experienced operators LOVE explaining their process. It validates their expertise.
3. Comments will reveal workflow details (speed ranges, test methods, documentation habits).
4. Multiple correct answers means lots of people can comment without feeling wrong.

**DM hook afterward:** "hey [name], your approach to [specific detail they mentioned] is smart. do you log those results somewhere or just remember what worked?"

---

### Post Option C: "Settings you'd pay money for"

```
If someone handed you a perfect, verified parameter sheet for ONE material on YOUR specific machine, what material would you pick?

Not "general starting points from the internet." I mean dialed-in, production-ready settings that you could run all day without babysitting.

What's the one material you'd pay money to never have to test again?
```

**Why this works:**
1. Forces people to name their highest-value/most-painful material.
2. "Pay money for" makes them think about it seriously, not just toss off a generic answer.
3. Every answer is literally a feature request for CutLog (tells you what to prioritize in the database).
4. Creates FOMO if others name a material they also struggle with.
5. Easy to DM: "hey, you mentioned [material]. actually built something that might help with that."

**DM hook afterward:** "hey [name], you said [material]. I've been building a tool that has data for that exact material. want me to send you what it recommends for your wattage?"
