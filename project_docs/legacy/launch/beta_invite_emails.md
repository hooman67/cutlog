# CutLog Beta Invite Email Templates

This document contains 7 email templates for the beta lifecycle: from waitlist signup through active user retention and re-engagement.

---

## Email #1: Welcome to Beta
**Send:** Immediately after signup  
**Goal:** Confirm they're in, set expectations, prime for participation

```
Subject: You're in the CutLog beta 🎉

Hi [First Name],

You're officially on the list. You made it to the beta.

Here's what happens next:
- We're rolling out beta access in waves starting [DATE]
- You'll get an email with a personal invite link
- Log in, set up your machine (takes 2 min), search a material, get a recommendation
- That's it. Just try it and let us know what's broken

In the meantime, share the waitlist with someone who'd find this useful:
[sharing link]

— Matt
CutLog
```

**Notes:**
- Use the checkmark/celebration emoji to feel earned, not generic
- "Made it to the beta" > "thanks for signing up" (scarcity + achievement)
- Set timeline expectations immediately (reduce anxiety about when access comes)
- Invite them to share (waitlist growth loop)

## Email #2: Beta Access Invite
**Send:** To first 50 waitlist signups (or first wave)  
**Goal:** Convert to activation (Settings → Search → Feedback)

```
Subject: Your CutLog beta access is ready

Hi [First Name],

Your invite is ready: https://cutlog-two.vercel.app

You're among the first 50 to get early access.

**Quick start (2 min):**
1. Go to Settings and tell us your machine (wattage, lens, brand)
2. Go to Recommend and search "Stainless Steel 3mm"
3. See the speed recommendation
4. Tell us if it's useful (or totally wrong)

**What we need from you:**
- Try 5 material searches
- Test one recommendation on your machine
- Give us honest feedback (the brutally honest kind)
- Reply to this email with what works/doesn't

This is real beta. Expect bugs. Expect half-finished features. That's the point.

— Matt
```

**Notes:**
- Embed the link inline (no button), copy-paste ready
- "Among the first 50" creates social proof + urgency
- Step-by-step onboarding (removes friction)
- Specific asks (5 searches, 1 test) prevent "read and forget"
- Normalize bugs/incompleteness (reduce expectations)

## Email #3: Feedback Request
**Send:** 1 week after invite sent  
**Goal:** Surface whether they used it, what worked, what didn't

```
Subject: Quick question: did CutLog help?

Hi [First Name],

It's been a week. How's CutLog treating you?

Three quick questions:
1. Did you try any recommendations on your machine?
2. If yes, were they in the ballpark?
3. What's the one thing we should fix first?

Just hit reply and let me know.

— Matt
```

**Notes:**
- Short window captures fresh memory
- 3 concrete questions beat open-ended "feedback?"
- Q2 assumes they tried it (presells adoption)
- Q3 gets actionable product feedback
- "One thing" prevents feedback paralysis

## Email #4: User Spotlight
**Send:** To active users after 10+ cuts logged  
**Goal:** Recognize power users, deepen engagement, source case studies

```
Subject: You're a CutLog power user 💪

Hi [First Name],

You've logged 10 cuts on CutLog. That's awesome.

We're looking for 3-4 operators to do a 30-min call and show us what they're building.

If you're interested, let me know a time that works.

Also: Can we feature your machine/use case on our site? (with your permission, of course)

— Matt
```

**Notes:**
- Calls out specific achievement (10 cuts) to show we're watching
- 💪 emoji for muscle/competence, not generic celebration
- Invite them into product decisions (deeper buy-in)
- Double ask: call + feature (gives two paths to say yes)
- Respect privacy (ask permission for feature)

## Email #5: Referral Bonus
**Send:** When user refers others and new referral signs up  
**Goal:** Reinforce network growth, show impact, incentivize more referrals

```
Subject: Someone joined because you shared CutLog

Hi [First Name],

[Referrer Name] just joined the beta thanks to your referral.

Not tracking "points" or anything (too gamified), but wanted you to know your word-of-mouth is working.

If you refer 2 more people, we'll give you [BONUS - pick one: lifetime free access, feature request priority, 1:1 training call]

— Matt
```

**Notes:**
- Personalize with referrer name (shows we track + care)
- "Word-of-mouth is working" validates their influence
- Anti-gamification note (credibility)
- Threshold bonus (2 more = 3 total, shows belief they know 2+ operators)
- Pick ONE bonus per company (keep promise manageable)

## Email #6: "You've Gone Quiet" Re-engagement
**Send:** After 2 weeks of no activity (no logins, no cuts logged)  
**Goal:** Understand drop-off, remove friction, salvage relationship

```
Subject: Come back? 👀

Hi [First Name],

Haven't seen you in a couple weeks. Did we break something?

Genuinely curious: what would make this tool useful for you?

Reply with anything: bugs, feature requests, or just "not for me" — all helpful.

— Matt
```

**Notes:**
- 👀 is casual, not demanding (lowers pressure)
- Assume ownership ("did we break it?" not "why'd you leave?")
- Open-ended question (genuine curiosity > sales pitch)
- "All helpful" removes judgment from negative feedback
- Short and low-effort (matches their low engagement)

## Email #7: Feature Announcement
**Send:** When shipping major updates (triggered by launches)  
**Goal:** Drive re-engagement with existing users, show momentum

```
Subject: CutLog now does [FEATURE]

Hi [First Name],

Quick ship update: We just launched [FEATURE] because beta users asked for it.

Try it and let us know if it's actually useful or if we missed the mark.

[Link to app]

— Matt
```

**Notes:**
- Lead with "because you asked" (validates their feedback)
- "Quick ship update" = momentum + responsiveness
- Invite skepticism ("missed the mark") builds trust
- One link, short copy (mobile-optimized)
- Triggers re-activation of inactive users

## Key Tone Guidelines

### Universal Rules
1. **Short**: All emails read in <30 seconds on mobile. Aim for 3-4 short paragraphs max.
2. **Personal**: Sign with first name only ("Matt"), not title or company.
3. **Honest**: Say "bugs," "beta," "expect issues." Don't oversell.
4. **Specific**: Ask for specific feedback ("did the speed work?") not generic ("thoughts?").
5. **Time-aware**: Show you value their time. Give time estimates (2 min, 30 min).

### Structure
- **Subject line**: Benefit or curiosity hook (no "CutLog Newsletter" generic stuff)
- **Greeting**: First name only
- **Body**: 1 ask or update. Not a brain dump.
- **CTA**: Clear action (reply, click link, try feature)
- **Signature**: First name + product name (— Matt / CutLog)

### Voice Checklist
- ✓ Conversational (would you say this in person?)
- ✓ No corporate speak ("leveraging," "synergy," "maximize ROI")
- ✓ No emoji overload (1-2 per email, strategic placement)
- ✓ No guilt-tripping ("we miss you," "come back")
- ✓ No fake urgency ("only 48 hours")
- ✓ Assume they're busy (respect their attention)

### Response Expectations
- **Reply goal**: 24 hours if they respond. Fast reply = investment signal.
- **Non-response**: Check in at 2 weeks. If still quiet, add to re-engagement list.
- **Negative feedback**: Say thank you. Use it immediately (shows they're heard).

## Implementation Notes

### Email Sequence Triggers
1. **Welcome to Beta** → On signup completion
2. **Beta Access Invite** → On wave 1 cohort deployment (first 50 users)
3. **Feedback Request** → 7 days after invite
4. **User Spotlight** → When cuts_logged >= 10
5. **Referral Bonus** → When referral_code returns a signup
6. **Re-engagement** → When last_login < 14 days AND cuts_logged > 0
7. **Feature Announcement** → On release of major features (manual trigger)

### Segmentation
- **Wave 1 (first 50)**: Send Email #2 immediately; stagger subsequent emails by 1-2 days
- **Later waves**: Send Email #1, then Email #2 per wave schedule
- **Active users**: Prioritize for features, spotlights, referrals
- **Inactive users**: Re-engagement focus; don't bombard with announcements

### A/B Testing Ideas
- Subject lines: emoji vs. no emoji
- CTA placement: inline link vs. button vs. text
- Tone: "we're watching you power user" vs. "you're doing great"
- Timing: 7 days vs. 10 days for feedback request

## Personalization Fields
- `[First Name]` — User's first name
- `[DATE]` — Wave access date (e.g., "June 20")
- `[Referrer Name]` — Name of person who referred them
- `[BONUS]` — Selected incentive (pick one per company cohort)
- `[FEATURE]` — Feature name from latest ship
- `[Link]` — Relevant app deep link (settings, recommend, specific feature)

All links should be tracked for engagement (UTM params or custom tracking).
